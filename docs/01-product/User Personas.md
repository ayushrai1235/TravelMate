## Persona 1: Rameshbhai Patel — The Pilgrimage Traveler

**Age:** 67  
**Location:** Navsari, Gujarat  
**Occupation:** Retired Government Employee  
**Income:** ₹35,000/month (pension)  
**Tech Literacy:** Low — uses WhatsApp, YouTube; struggles with multiple apps  
**Language:** Gujarati, basic Hindi

### Background
Rameshbhai visits at least 4 temples per year — Trimbakeshwar, Somnath, Dwarka, Shirdi. He has been doing this for decades, but now finds it increasingly difficult to coordinate travel as he is alone more often. His children live in different cities and can't always help plan. He has used IRCTC but finds the UI confusing.

### Goals
- Get to the temple on time for morning aarti (specific time constraint)
- Find a clean, affordable dharamshala or hotel near the temple
- Avoid busy or risky routes
- Know the crowd level before going during festivals
- Have a simple printout or offline guide he can show to auto drivers

### Pain Points
- Cannot manage 5 different apps simultaneously
- Confusing IRCTC UI, often makes booking mistakes
- Unsure about last-mile transport from railway station to temple
- Misses aartis because of poor planning
- Depends on tour operators who overcharge

### How TravelMate AI Helps
- Single input: "Navsari → Trimbakeshwar" — full itinerary generated
- Temple timings prominently displayed
- Dharamshala/budget hotel options within walking distance
- Large text mode, high contrast UI
- Offline PDF generation for showing to auto drivers
- Voice assistant in Gujarati (v2.0)

### Device
- Android smartphone (mid-range), 4G connection
- Uses phone primarily for calls and WhatsApp

## Persona 2: Priya Sharma — The Solo Millennial Traveler

**Age:** 28  
**Location:** Pune, Maharashtra  
**Occupation:** UX Designer at a tech startup  
**Income:** ₹1.2 lakh/month  
**Tech Literacy:** High — power user of multiple apps  
**Language:** English, Hindi, Marathi

### Background
Priya takes 2–3 solo trips per year across India. She enjoys discovering new destinations, trying local food, and sharing experiences on Instagram. She is budget-conscious but will pay for convenience. She has tried Google Travel, Rome2rio, and various travel blogs, but finds herself manually stitching together information from multiple sources.

### Goals
- Discover optimal routes she might not have thought of
- Get food recommendations at every stop
- Budget her trip in advance
- Find the safest route for solo female travel
- Export a clean itinerary to share with family for safety

### Pain Points
- Spends 3+ hours planning each trip across multiple apps
- Rome2rio gives global data but not accurate Indian data
- Google Maps doesn't plan inter-city multi-modal well
- No single source for budget estimation
- Safety information scattered across blogs

### How TravelMate AI Helps
- Complete door-to-door plan in one search
- Food suggestions at each transit point
- Budget breakdown per leg
- AI chat to refine the itinerary
- PDF export with complete details

### Device
- iPhone 15 (primary)
- MacBook Pro (secondary for planning)

## Persona 3: The Sharma Family — Family Group Travelers

**Family Composition:** Parents (42, 40), two children (14, 9)  
**Location:** Ahmedabad, Gujarat  
**Occupation:** Father — engineer; Mother — schoolteacher  
**Income:** ₹2.5 lakh/month combined  
**Tech Literacy:** Medium  
**Language:** Gujarati, Hindi, English

### Background
The Sharma family plans 1–2 major trips per year, typically during school vacations. They travel to religious destinations and hill stations. Key constraints: children's patience, comfort of elderly grandparent who sometimes joins, vegetarian food requirement, and budget management.

### Goals
- Plan a route suitable for children and possible elderly member
- Find family-friendly hotels (connected rooms, vegetarian kitchen nearby)
- Avoid very crowded times
- Budget total trip cost including food
- Plan activity stops for children at tourist attractions en route

### Pain Points
- Managing multiple tickets and schedules for a group
- Finding child-friendly accommodation and food
- Keeping children engaged during long transit legs
- Cost overruns when unexpected expenses arise

### How TravelMate AI Helps
- Group size input adjusts recommendations
- Family-friendly hotel filters
- Vegetarian food markers on suggestions
- Budget calculator for the whole group
- Kid-friendly POI suggestions along route

## Persona 4: Anjali Kumar — The Business Traveler

**Age:** 35  
**Location:** Mumbai, Maharashtra  
**Occupation:** Sales Manager, FMCG company  
**Income:** ₹3.5 lakh/month  
**Tech Literacy:** High

- Expense export (CSV / PDF) per leg

## Persona 5: Carlos Mendoza — The Foreign Tourist

**Age:** 31  
**Location:** Originally Barcelona, Spain; visiting India for 3 weeks  
**Occupation:** Software Engineer (remote worker)  
**Income:** €4,000/month  
**Tech Literacy:** High  
**Language:** English, Spanish; no Hindi/Gujarati

### Background
Carlos is visiting India for the first time — a "Golden Triangle + Rajasthan + Kerala" route. He is comfortable with technology but completely unfamiliar with Indian transport systems, payment methods, and cultural norms. He needs clear guidance on every step.

### Goals
- Understand the complete transport network for his route
- Know what to expect at each step (which platform, which gate, etc.)
- Have all information in English
- Know costs in both INR and EUR
- Know what documents to carry (train PNR, etc.)
- Feel safe in unfamiliar territory

### Pain Points
- Cannot read Hindi on signs
- Unfamiliar with IRCTC, auto negotiation, local buses
- Unsure about appropriate prices (fear of being overcharged)
- Overwhelmed by complexity of Indian transport

### How TravelMate AI Helps
- English-only interface with simplified explanations
- "What to expect" notes at each transition ("At Nashik Road station, exit from Platform 1, turn right for auto stand")
- Price context ("₹150 auto ride is fair for this distance")
- Currency display in EUR alongside INR
- Emergency assistance with local police/embassy contacts
- Cultural notes (temple dress code, offering etiquette)

**Age:** 22  
**Location:** Chennai, Tamil Nadu  
**Occupation:** Engineering student, IIT Madras  
**Income:** ₹8,000/month (stipend + allowance)  
**Tech Literacy:** Very High  
**Language:** English, Tamil, Hindi

### Background
Vikram takes 2–3 backpacker trips per year during semester breaks, always on the tightest possible budget. He travels by sleeper class trains, state buses, and hitchhiking. He plans trips obsessively in advance using multiple sources.

### Goals

- Know all budget options (general class, sleeper, state bus)

- Find hostels and free camping spots
- Connect with other backpackers

### Pain Points
- Time-consuming to compare all budget options
- Fear of missing the cheapest option
- Finding affordable food at transit points
- Real-time train delay information

### How TravelMate AI Helps
- Budget-first route optimization
- Cheapest transport class explicitly shown
- Budget accommodation filters (hostels, dharamshalas)
- Real-time delay data to catch transfers
- Community trip sharing (v2.0)
